About Haggly

Breaking language barriers in local commerce

What Is Haggly?

Haggly is a free, open-source negotiation phrase translator built for people who buy and sell on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, OfferUp, and other local marketplaces. It gives you instant access to common negotiation phrases in 18 languages ‚Äî ready to copy and paste into any messaging app.

Unlike general-purpose translators, Haggly is designed specifically for marketplace conversations. Every phrase is crafted for the context of buying and selling: asking about condition, negotiating price, arranging pickup, and closing the deal.

Why We Built This

In diverse communities across the United States and around the world, marketplace listings appear in dozens of languages. A great deal on a couch might be posted in Spanish. A rare collectible might be listed in Vietnamese. A bulk electronics lot might be described in Mandarin.

Most buyers skip these listings entirely ‚Äî not because the items aren't good, but because the language feels like a wall. Haggly tears down that wall. We believe great deals shouldn't be limited by the language you speak.

At the same time, sellers in non-English-speaking communities often struggle to reach the full local market. Haggly helps them too, by making it easy for buyers to communicate in the seller's preferred language.

Who Is Haggly For?

  • Buyers who want to access more listings and negotiate confidently in any language
  • Sellers who want to reach a broader local audience regardless of language
  • Resellers and flippers who browse marketplace listings across multiple language communities
  • New immigrants who are still learning English but want to participate in local commerce
  • Anyone who's ever stared at a great deal in another language and wished they could just say "Is this still available?"

Our Principles

  • Free forever. Haggly's core phrase translator will always be free to use.
  • No accounts required. We don't collect personal data or require sign-ups.
  • Privacy first. We don't track what you translate or who you negotiate with.
  • Open and honest. Our translations are reviewed for accuracy in real marketplace contexts.
